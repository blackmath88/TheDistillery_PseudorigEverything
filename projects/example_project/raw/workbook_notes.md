# Workbook Notes — Knowledge Distillation

## Exercise 1: What do you actually know?

Write down everything you know about your topic area without looking anything up.

My answer:
I know that knowledge management is a field that's been around since the 90s.
I know that most personal knowledge management (PKM) tools are too complex for most people.
I know that the bottleneck is usually input, not retrieval — people don't capture enough.
I know that AI tools like LLMs are changing the retrieval side dramatically.
I know that the missing piece is structured, clean input data.

## Exercise 2: What formats do your notes currently live in?

- Voice memos (transcribed): txt files
- Course notes: md files
- Reading highlights: PDF annotations (sometimes exported)
- Random thoughts: txt files, sometimes directly in md
- Video transcripts: txt files from YouTube auto-captions

## Exercise 3: What would "done" look like?

A folder structure where every piece of knowledge I've consumed is:
1. Extracted into clean text
2. Tagged with source type and project
3. Chunked and ready for vector embedding
4. Summarised at a project level

## Exercise 4: What's the simplest version of this?

The simplest version:
- One script
- One input folder
- Five output files
- No databases, no APIs, no infrastructure

That's the MVP. That's The Distillery.
